DamerauLevenshteinDissimilarity()

Damerau-Levenshtein dissimilarity is the loss metric pendant to the DamerauLevenshteinDistance. It measures the percentage of single-character edits (insertions, deletions, or substitutions) and transpositions of adjacent characters that are necessary to change one text into another, among the number of characters of the longer of both texts. Damerau-Levenshtein dissimilarity always evaluates to heuristic values within the interval 0,1. Texts that evaluate to greater heuristic values are considered to be less similar than texts that evaluate to smaller heuristic values.
